D Vitamin

Vitamin D, also known as the "sunlight vitamin", is one of the most important vitamins for the body. It regulates calcium-phosphorus metabolism, ensures normal blood clotting, as well as the growth and development of bones for children and the strengthening of bone density for adults. It is used in the prevention of osteoporosis, is involved in the regulation of blood pressure and heart function, and blocks the uncontrolled multiplication of body cells, which makes it effective in the prevention of cancer. Also, it plays an important role in regulating mood, preventing chronic fatigue, anxiety and depression.

K2 Vitamin

K2 Vitamin is involved in blood clotting and regulates cell function, slowing aging in adults and supporting healthy growth in children. It improves bone and dental health, reduces the risk of inflammation, and helps prevent osteoporosis, diabetes, fractures, cardiovascular and autoimmune diseases, and some cancers. In addition, vitamin K2 plays a very important role in the development of a correct face shape, strong bones, primary teeth in the embryo, therefore, it is recommended for expectant mothers.

Magnesium

Magnesium is one of the most essential macronutrients for the stable functioning of the heart. He also participates in the work of the nervous system, immunity, affects mental health and helps the production of more than three hundred enzymes. Thanks to this macronutrient, the production of energy necessary for normal life occurs. Magnesium also benefits many body systems: it improves heart rate, optimizes blood pressure, lowers bad cholesterol, lowers the risk of stroke and type 2 diabetes, participates in glucose metabolism, and also reduces fluid retention in the body.

C Vitamin

C Vitamin C strengthens the immune system, and also protects it from viruses and bacteria, accelerates the healing process of wounds, affects the synthesis of a number of hormones, regulates hematopoiesis and normalizes capillary permeability, participates in the synthesis of collagen protein, which is necessary for the growth of tissue cells.
